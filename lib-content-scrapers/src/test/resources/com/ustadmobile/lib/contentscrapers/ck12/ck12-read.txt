                  <p id="x-ck12-YTE3NmY0MGNlY2YwYzllOTQxMzJiMjgwZDdjZGYwZjY.-xnw"><strong>What affects <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Population" href="/c/biology/population" target="_blank">population</a> growth in the United States?</strong></p>
                  <p id="x-ck12-NDFlZTdmZWRlMzM4OGNkNDk5MTgwZTJjZjM4MmRhNzg.-zys">One way the <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Population" href="/c/biology/population" target="_blank">population</a> of the United States has grown is through the movement of individuals into the United States from other parts of the world. The same effects can be seen in wildlife populations. Individuals move into a population and increase its size. This is just one of the many factors affecting population growth. Of course, individuals may also move away from a population.</p>
                  <h3 id="x-ck12-UG9wdWxhdGlvbiBHcm93dGg.">Population Growth</h3>
                  <p id="x-ck12-N2IwZGNiZjEwNjJjNjg5N2RkNTg1ZDM2NDVmMWY1M2U.-f4y">What does population growth mean? You can probably guess that it means the number of individuals in a population is increasing. The <strong><span class="vocab_term">population growth rate</span></strong> tells you how quickly a population is increasing or decreasing. What determines the population growth rate for a particular population?</p>
                  <h4 id="x-ck12-QmlydGhzLCBEZWF0aHMsIGFuZCBNaWdyYXRpb24.">Births, Deaths, and <span class="vocab_term">Migration</span></h4>
                  <p id="x-ck12-NWRiZWI2NjhiM2M4MTliODY2ZGZkN2ZlZWE0ZTAzYmE.-wxb">Population growth rate depends on birth rates and death rates, as well as <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Migration" href="/c/biology/migration" target="_blank">migration</a>. First, we will consider the effects of birth and death rates. You can predict the growth rate by using this simple equation: <em>growth rate = birth rate – death rate.</em></p>
                  <p id="x-ck12-MDM3N2RlZjllM2I3NDE4ZjRjMzBiNzcxOWVkN2Q2MjM.-wt6">If the birth rate is larger than the death rate, then the population grows. If the death rate is larger than the birth rate, what will happen to the population? The population size will decrease. If the birth and death rates are equal, then the population size will not change.</p>
                  <p id="x-ck12-YzJkNDc4MjdlMDlhZWVhM2UwODAyYzg5NThhOTQ5YjA.-szt">Factors that affect population growth are:</p>
                  <ol id="x-ck12-NjcyNzZmNTVhNThjMGM2ODQ0MWRiMmZiNDU5ODVhZmI.-nrf">
                   <li>Age of organisms at first <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Reproduction" href="/c/biology/reproduction" target="_blank">reproduction</a>.</li>
                   <li>How often an organism reproduces.</li>
                   <li>The number of offspring of an organism.</li>
                   <li>The presence or absence of <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Parental Care" href="/c/biology/parental-care" target="_blank">parental care</a>.</li>
                   <li>How long an organism is able to reproduce.</li>
                   <li>The death rate of offspring.</li>
                  </ol>
                  <p id="x-ck12-MDE0M2U3NTUyNTM2YjlhMDg5MGE5ZjM2MTZmOWI4ODg.-idw">For an ecosystem to be stable, populations in that system must be healthy, and that usually means reproducing as much as their environment allows. Do organisms reproduce yearly or every few years? Do organisms reproduce for much of their life, or just part of their life? Do organisms produce many offspring at once, or just a few, or even just one? Do many newborn organisms die, or do the majority survive? All these factors play a role in the growth of a population.</p>
                  <p id="x-ck12-MzBjZTI4NThhNmUyMjRjZWEzOGQ1NDJjZTM4MGMwM2I.-3j9">Organisms can use different strategies to increase their <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Reproduction" href="/c/biology/reproduction" target="_blank">reproduction</a> rate. <strong><span class="vocab_term">Altricial</span></strong> organisms are helpless at birth, and their parents give them a lot of care. This care is often seen in bird <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Species" href="/c/biology/species" target="_blank">species</a>. (<strong>Figure</strong> <a href="#x-ck12-TVNMUy0yMy0wNi1yZXByb2R1Y3RpdmUtc3RyYXRlZ2llcw..">below</a>). Altricial <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Birds" href="/c/biology/birds" target="_blank">birds</a> are usually born blind and without feathers. Compared to <span class="vocab_term">precocial</span> organisms, altricial organisms have a longer period of <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Development" href="/c/biology/development" target="_blank">development</a> before they reach maturity. <strong>Precocial</strong> organisms, such as the geese shown below, can take care of themselves at birth and do not require help from their parents (<strong>Figure</strong> <a href="#x-ck12-TVNMUy0yMy0wNi1yZXByb2R1Y3RpdmUtc3RyYXRlZ2llcw..">below</a>). In order to reproduce as much as possible, altricial and precocial organisms must use very different strategies.</p>

                  <h4 id="x-ck12-TWlncmF0aW9u">Migration</h4>
                  <p id="x-ck12-ZDZlMDBkYWQ0N2I3ODc3ZjFjODE3MzgxMGFjNDZjYWI.-lpz"><strong>Migration</strong> is the movement of individual organisms into, or out of, a population. <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Migration" href="/c/biology/migration" target="_blank">Migration</a> affects population growth rate. There are two types of migration:</p>
                  <ol id="x-ck12-NTFhMzgxZTkzZmY4YjZkN2ViYTBkOWYzOTk1M2M5NTQ.-xn5">
                   <li><strong><span class="vocab_term">Immigration</span></strong> is the movement of individuals into a population from other areas. This increases the population size and growth rate.</li>
                   <li><strong><span class="vocab_term">Emigration</span></strong> is the movement of individuals out of a population. This decreases the population size and growth rate.</li>
                  </ol>
                  <p id="x-ck12-NjAyYjQ0OGEyNTk4N2VlM2QxYmE1ZGNmMjdlZDY4YWY.-8on">The earlier growth rate equation can be modified to account for <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Migration" href="/c/biology/migration" target="_blank">migration</a>: <em>growth rate = (birth rate + immigration rate) – (death rate + emigration rate).</em></p>
                  <p id="x-ck12-YWFiNjhjODQ1OGU1YmVlOGNlYjQ4Y2U0ZTFhMDY4Njk.-czo">One type of migration that you are probably familiar with is the migration of <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Birds" href="/c/biology/birds" target="_blank">birds</a>. Maybe you have heard that birds fly south for the winter. In the fall, birds fly thousands of miles to the south where it is warmer. In the spring, they return to their homes. (<strong>Figure</strong> <a href="#x-ck12-TVNMUy0yMy0wOC1iYXJuYWNsZS1nZWVzZS1taWdyYXRpb24.">below</a>).</p>
                  <p id="x-ck12-OWYzZWJiYzM4YzJlYzFmNmU0MTg1NDQyMTk5MjhlOGM.-y5w">Monarch butterflies also migrate from Mexico to the northern U.S. in the summer and back to Mexico in the winter. These types of migrations move entire populations from one <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Location" href="/c/earth-science/location" target="_blank">location</a> to another.</p>

                  <h4 id="x-ck12-RXhwb25lbnRpYWwgR3Jvd3Ro"><span class="vocab_term">Exponential Growth</span></h4>
                  <p id="x-ck12-NmUzZmQxNzQ2N2I2MTBlZWIwY2EzZGFlY2E3YzBiMTI.-sbp">Population growth can be described with two models, based on the size of the population and necessary <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Resources" href="/c/biology/resources" target="_blank">resources</a>. These two types of growth are known as exponential growth and <span class="vocab_term">logistic growth</span>. If a population is given unlimited amounts of resources, such as food and <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Water" href="/c/biology/water" target="_blank">water</a>, land if needed, moisture, oxygen, and other environmental factors, it will grow exponentially. <strong>Exponential growth</strong> occurs as a population grows larger, dramatically increasing the growth rate. This is shown as a "J-shaped" curve below (<strong>Figure</strong> <a href="#x-ck12-TVNMUy0yMy0wOS1wb3B1bGF0aW9uLWdyb3d0aC1ncmFwaHM.">below</a>). You can see that the population grows slowly at first, but as time passes, growth occurs more and more rapidly.</p>
                  <div id="x-ck12-YTliNjBhZDcxOWQ5ZDU3MTdhMzc3NDgwMjRlNjc0NTc.-uyk" class="x-ck12-img-postcard x-ck12-nofloat image-with-caption block-image">

                  <p id="x-ck12-ZjJmMTk1ODUxYjI5NTNkNTFlZWE4MzRjZTc5NzdmNTI.-m8z">In nature, organisms do not usually have ideal environments with unlimited food. In nature, there are limits. Sometimes, there will be plenty of food. Sometimes, a fire will wipe out all of the available nutrients. Sometimes a predator will kill many individuals in a population. How do you think these limits affect the way organisms grow?</p>
                  <p id="x-ck12-M2Q3NmExNjk1YTI2NGU2YzEyYjYzYTFhNTk3ZTVjZjI.-tlf">Usually, populations first grow exponentially while <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="Resources" href="/c/biology/resources" target="_blank">resources</a> are abundant. But as populations increase and resources become less available, rates of growth slow down and slowly level off, reaching the <span class="vocab_term">carrying capacity</span>. The <strong>carrying capacity</strong> is the upper limit to the population size that <a class="x-ck12-crossref" title="The Environment" href="/c/chemistry/the-environment" target="_blank">the environment</a> can support. This type of growth is shown as an "S-shaped" curve below (<strong>Figure</strong> <a href="#x-ck12-TVNMUy0yMy0wOS1wb3B1bGF0aW9uLWdyb3d0aC1ncmFwaHM.">above</a>) and is called <strong>logistic growth.</strong> Why do you think occurs?</p>
                  <h3 id="x-ck12-U3VtbWFyeQ..">Summary</h3>
                  <ul id="x-ck12-YjJiMTI0YzE0ODY1M2E5OGI0NmY5NDM0YzI0NzQ5NWE.-8wd">
                   <li>Population growth rate is affected by birth rates, death rates, immigration, and emigration.</li>
                   <li>If a population is given unlimited amounts of food, moisture, and oxygen, and other environmental factors, it will show exponential growth.</li>
                  </ul>
